Airbnb in the Greater Melbourne Area

Airbnb is a popular website where travellers can browse and book private rooms, hotel rooms, shared rooms, and even entire homes and apartments to stay in, and owners can list their homes for others. Airbnb listings can be found all over the world, but this visualisation will focus on those in the Greater Melbourne Area.

Where are the listings located?

The Greater Melbourne Area is currently home to over 23,000 listings on Airbnb. Let's take a look at where they're located:

The City of Melbourne is both the most Airbnb-dense area, and the local council area with the most Airbnb listings. This is likely due to its central location and convenience for travellers using Airbnb. The City of Casey is the local council area with both the greatest number of dwellings and the lowest density of listings.

In general, the areas closer to the Melbourne Central Business District (CBD) have a higher density of Airbnbs compared to those further away. However, despite being futher from the CBD, there is a high density of Airbnb listings in the Yarra Ranges, likely due to its popularity as a tourist destination.

What kind of listings are they?

For most Local Council Areas, the most common type of room listed on airbnb is entire homes and apartments, making up 72.8% of all listings in Greater Melbourne. Private rooms are often the second most common (25.6%), while shared rooms (1.2%) and hotel rooms (0.4%) are far less common.

However, for some areas, such as Whittlesea and Greater Dandenong, private rooms are more common, making up 56.98% and 59.30% of listings in those areas, respectively.


How much do they cost, and how are they rated?

The average prices of different areas vary greatly, ranging from $123 a night to more than triple that at $390 a night. In general, the higher the average price of an area, the higher the rating of the listings in that area.

As shown in the bubble plot below, the average nightly price of entire homes and apartments ($261.61 across all local council areas) tends to be more expensive than private rooms ($150.73). Shared rooms are much cheaper with an average of $94.00 a night, while hotel rooms ($243.19) are nearly as expensive as entire homes and apartments.

What sets the different listing types apart?

Airbnb listings vary greatly in terms of price, rating, number of rooms, and minimum length of your stay. Let's take a look at some of the common trends between the different listing types:

Unsurprisingly, the numbers of bedrooms, beds, and bathrooms tend to correlate strongly with each other. Interestingly, rating tends to inversely proportional to price, meaning lower prices tend to get higher ratings and vice versa. This could be due to travellers feeling that they got better value for money in less expensive homestays.

Across nearly all Local Council Areas, entire homes and apartments tend to have more bathrooms, more beds, more bathrooms, higher ratings, and therefore higher prices when compared to private rooms. Due to small sample sizes, it is difficult to make generalizations about shared rooms and hotel rooms.
